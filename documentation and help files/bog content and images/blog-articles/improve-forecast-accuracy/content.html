About Ready Signal 

Ready Signal is revolutionizing how businesses harness external data to solve problems they didn’t know could be fixed. Our platform transforms raw, chaotic data into clear, actionable insights—driving efficiency, growth, and smarter decision-making for businesses worldwide. Learn More About Ready Signal

Meet Our Expert Speakers

Jason Harper

Jason Harper, the visionary Founder of Ready Signal, is revolutionizing how businesses harness external data to solve problems they didn’t know could be fixed. With Ready Signal, Jason has pioneered a game-changing approach that slashes forecasting errors by up to 50%, empowering companies to finally grasp the critical impact of market conditions, weather, population trends, and more on their bottom line. Known for his creative problem-solving and knack for turning obstacles into opportunities, he brings over 20 years of trailblazing experience in analytics, machine learning, and AI. 

Armed with multiple degrees in Economics and an MBA from the University of Michigan’s Ross School of Business, Jason fuses technical brilliance with strategic foresight. His innovative spirit has birthed two patented machine learning technologies and a platform that transforms raw, chaotic data into clear, actionable insights—driving efficiency, growth, and smarter decision-making for businesses worldwide. 

A recognized thought leader, Jason’s insights have appeared in the Wall Street Journal, MIT Technology Review, Ad Age and more. By balancing cutting-edge innovation with tangible real-world impact, he’s unlocking new potential for companies ready to stop guessing and start knowing. 

Steve Bartos, PhD

Steve is currently the Senior Director of Corporate Data & Analytics at The Worthington Steel Company, headquartered in Columbus, Ohio.  In this role, he is responsible for driving impactful analytics across the enterprise through strong collaboration, transformative project work, and a strategic focus on enablement and empowerment. 

Steve joined Worthington Industries 10 years ago as an Advanced Analytics Analyst in the Steel Division, where he was tasked with expanding the use of data and analytics across the enterprise. He later moved to a managerial role on the Steel Analytics Team and eventually succeeded Worthington Steel’s current CIO Bill Wertz as the second leader of the Corporate Data & Analytics Team. 

Prior to joining Worthington, Steve was a high school mathematics and physics teacher in Portage, IN for 13 years after graduating from Purdue University. He later earned his PhD in Science Education from the Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago, Illinois, and subsequently joined the faculty of Middle Tennessee State University, where he taught in both the undergraduate and doctoral programs.
